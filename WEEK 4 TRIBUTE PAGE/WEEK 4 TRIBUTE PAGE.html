Alan Turing

The father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence

Timeline of Turing's Legacy:

  • 1912 - Born in London, England.
  • 1936 - Published "On Computable Numbers," introducing the concept of the Universal Turing Machine.
  • 1939 - Joined Bletchley Park to lead the effort in cracking Nazi Germany's Enigma code.
  • 1945 - Awarded the OBE for his vital contributions to the war effort.
  • 1950 - Proposed the "Turing Test" to measure machine intelligence.
  • 2013 - Officially pardoned by Queen Elizabeth II.

Turing's work wasn't just about math; it was about the fundamental nature of what it means to calculate and think.
